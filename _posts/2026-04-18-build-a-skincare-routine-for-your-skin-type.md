## Introduction
Your skin type determines everything — the products you choose, the ingredients you use, and how your skin responds to your routine. This guide breaks down exactly how to build a personalized routine based on your skin’s unique needs.

## Step 1: Identify Your Skin Type

Before you can build a routine that truly works, you need to understand what your skin is naturally trying to tell you. The easiest way to do that is with a quick at‑home test that reveals how your skin behaves without any products influencing it.

### 🔍 The Bare‑Face Test

Follow this simple method:

1. **Cleanse your face** with a gentle, non‑stripping cleanser to remove makeup, sunscreen, and surface oils.  
2. **Pat dry** with a soft towel. Don’t apply anything afterward — no toner, no moisturizer, no serums.  
3. **Wait 30 minutes** and let your skin return to its natural state.

### 🧭 What to Look For

After half an hour, check in with how your skin *looks* and *feels*. These small cues reveal a lot:

- **Shiny or slick — especially in the T‑zone → Oily Skin**  
  Skin may look glossy, feel greasy to the touch, or show more visible pores.

- **Tight, rough, or slightly flaky → Dry Skin**  
  You might feel an urge to moisturize, and fine lines may appear more noticeable.

- **Shiny in the T‑zone but dry or tight on the cheeks → Combination Skin**  
  This is extremely common — oilier center, drier perimeter.

- **Comfortable, balanced, and not overly shiny or tight → Normal Skin**  
  Skin feels soft and even, with minimal visible pores and no areas of discomfort.

### 📝 Optional: The Blotting Sheet Test

If you want extra confirmation, gently press a blotting sheet onto different areas of your face — forehead, nose, chin, and cheeks — after the 30‑minute bare‑face wait. Hold it up to the light and look at how much oil shows through.

Here’s what your results mean:

- **Oil on most areas → Oily Skin**  
  The sheet will look noticeably translucent in several spots. This means your skin produces more sebum throughout the day and benefits from lightweight, oil‑balancing products.

- **Little to no oil anywhere → Dry Skin**  
  The sheet stays mostly matte. This indicates your skin isn’t producing enough oil on its own and needs richer, barrier‑supporting hydration.

- **Oil only in the T‑zone (forehead, nose, chin) → Combination Skin**  
  The center of the sheet will show oil, while the cheek areas stay dry. This means you may need different textures for different zones — lightweight in the T‑zone, more moisture on the cheeks.

- **Light, even oil across the sheet → Normal Skin**  
  A small amount of oil shows up, but nothing excessive. This suggests your skin is balanced, comfortable, and not prone to extremes.

This quick test is a great way to double‑check your skin type — especially if your skin behaves differently depending on the season or your current routine.

## Step 2: Build Your Routine Based on Your Skin Type

### ⭐ Routine for Oily Skin
**Goals:** Control shine, prevent clogged pores, balance oil  
**Best ingredients:** Niacinamide, salicylic acid, clay, lightweight hydrators  

**AM Routine**
1. Gel cleanser  
2. Niacinamide serum  
3. Oil‑free moisturizer  
4. Sunscreen  

**PM Routine**
1. Cleanser  
2. BHA exfoliant (2–3x/week)  
3. Lightweight moisturizer  


### ⭐ Routine for Dry Skin
**Goals:** Hydrate, repair barrier, reduce flakiness  
**Best ingredients:** Hyaluronic acid, ceramides, squalane, glycerin  

**AM Routine**
1. Cream cleanser  
2. Hydrating serum  
3. Rich moisturizer  
4. Sunscreen  

**PM Routine**
1. Gentle cleanser  
2. Hydrating serum  
3. Thick night cream or oil  

### ⭐ Routine for Combination Skin
**Goals:** Balance oily and dry areas  
**Best ingredients:** Niacinamide, hyaluronic acid, lightweight lotions  

**AM Routine**
1. Gentle cleanser  
2. Hydrating serum  
3. Lightweight moisturizer  
4. Sunscreen  

**PM Routine**
1. Cleanser  
2. Serum (hydrating or balancing)  
3. Moisturizer  

### ⭐ Routine for Sensitive Skin
**Goals:** Soothe, reduce redness, avoid irritation  
**Best ingredients:** Aloe, centella, ceramides, oat extract  

**AM Routine**
1. Very gentle cleanser  
2. Soothing serum  
3. Barrier‑repair moisturizer  
4. Mineral sunscreen  

**PM Routine**
1. Cleanser  
2. Moisturizer  

> Avoid fragrance, strong acids, and harsh scrubs.

### ⭐ Routine for Acne‑Prone Skin
**Goals:** Unclog pores, reduce inflammation  
**Best ingredients:** Salicylic acid, benzoyl peroxide, niacinamide  

**AM Routine**
1. Cleanser  
2. Niacinamide  
3. Lightweight moisturizer  
4. Sunscreen  

**PM Routine**
1. Cleanser  
2. BHA or benzoyl peroxide  
3. Moisturizer  

## FAQs

**1. Can I mix products from different brands?**  
Yes — mix and match freely.

**2. How long should I wait between steps?**  
Usually 30–60 seconds.

**3. Do I need toner?**  
Only if it serves a purpose (hydration or exfoliation).

## Conclusion
Building a skincare routine tailored to your skin type is the foundation of healthy, balanced skin. By understanding your skin’s unique needs and choosing ingredients that support its natural functions, you can create a routine that works *with* your skin — not against it. Consistency is key: stick to your regimen, adjust seasonally, and always listen to how your skin responds. Over time, you’ll notice improved texture, radiance, and resilience — the true signs of a well‑crafted skincare routine.
